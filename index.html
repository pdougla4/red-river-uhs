Red River Hydrogen Storage: Operating Envelope and CO₂-EOR Transition

Safe injection operating envelopes for underground hydrogen storage in the Red River Formation, Williston Basin — constrained by transient reservoir modeling, laboratory characterization, and uncertainty quantification.

Project Overview

Objective

Deliver an operator-ready Safe Operating Envelope (SOE) in injection rate (q) vs. temperature differential (ΔT) space for underground hydrogen storage in the Red River Formation, using the Stecker 23-3 well as the primary data anchor.

Safety Framework

Four pseudo-coupled safety indices govern the envelope: AIG (Anhydrite Integrity Guard), RMG (Reservoir Mechanical Guard), and PGN (capillary seal / fingering stability). A No-False-Green policy enforces P90 conservative boundaries — regions near thresholds derate to amber or red.

Modeling Stack

Warren-Root dual-porosity + heat kernel (Python) as the primary engine; CMG/GEM for validation anchors. Uncertainty quantification via LHS → Morris screening → Sobol analysis on a reduced parameter set.

CO₂-EOR Transition

A secondary sub-project evaluates the Red River Formation as a dual-use target: underground hydrogen storage paired with CO₂-enhanced oil recovery. CMG/GEM compositional simulation anchors the EOR component.
